title: Checkpoints
description: What Varys watches for changes.
---

A **checkpoint** is a screenshot Varys takes during a test and watches for changes. A test can
have as many as you like — mark a checkpoint wherever you want to be sure a screen still looks
right.

## What to capture

When you add a checkpoint, you choose how much to capture:

<Cards>
  <Card title="Full page" description="The whole screen — best for 'does this page still render?'" />
  <Card title="Element" description="A single component, like a chart or a card." />
  <Card title="Region" description="A rectangle you draw yourself." />
</Cards>

## Hide noisy areas

Some things change every time on their own — clocks, dates, random ids — and would trigger false
alarms. Paint a **mask** over those areas and Varys ignores them when comparing.

## How picky a check is

Tiny rendering differences (a pixel of anti-aliasing) shouldn't count as a change. Each
checkpoint has a **sensitivity** you can adjust: loosen it to ignore trivial noise, or tighten it
to catch the smallest difference. You can tune both masks and sensitivity right from the review
screen and try them instantly.
