---
title: Reviewing changes
description: Comparing before and after, and deciding what to do.
---

When a run finds a difference, you compare the approved version against the new one. A few view
modes help you spot subtle changes:

- **Side by side** — the two images next to each other
- **Overlay** — the difference highlighted on top of the image
- **Swipe** and **blink** — slide between or flip back and forth, good for catching small shifts

## Approve or reject

- **Approve** — the new look is correct, so it becomes the baseline going forward. This is
  permanent: the old baseline is replaced and can't be brought back, so Varys asks you to confirm.
- **Reject** — the change is a real problem to fix.

If a run has several flagged checkpoints and they're all fine, you can approve them all at once.

<Callout type="info">
  Getting false alarms from areas that always change, or from tiny rendering noise? You can adjust
  [masks and sensitivity](/docs/checkpoints) right here and re-check instantly.
</Callout>
