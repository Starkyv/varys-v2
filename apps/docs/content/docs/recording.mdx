title: Recording with the extension
description: Capture a flow by hand with the Chrome recorder.
---

The Varys Chrome extension follows along as you use your app and turns your clicks, typing, and
navigation into a test. (Install it from [Local setup](/docs/getting-started#load-the-recorder-extension).)

## Record a flow

<Steps>

<Step>
### Open the panel
Click the **Varys icon** in the toolbar. A small panel appears on the page and stays put while
you work.
</Step>

<Step>
### Start recording
Press **Start recording**. Now Varys captures every click and what you type as you go through
your app — even across page loads and logins. The panel shows a running count.
</Step>

<Step>
### Mark checkpoints
A checkpoint is a screenshot Varys will watch for changes. Choose what to capture, press
**📷 Capture**, then click the thing you want:

- **Element** — a single component
- **Region** — a rectangle you draw
- **Full page** — the whole screen

You can capture as many as you like. After picking a target, you can paint over areas that
change on their own (like timestamps) so they're ignored.
</Step>

<Step>
### Name and save
Give the test a name and press **Save**.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout>
  Saving just stores the test. To actually check it, open the app's **Tests** tab and press
  **Run** — the first run sets the baseline you approve.
</Callout>

## What Varys handles for you

So a test works against any environment (staging, production, local), Varys automatically keeps
the web address and passwords out of the test. Everything else you type into a form stays with
that test by default — so filling one test's form never affects another. If a value should instead
change between environments, you can promote it to an environment variable in one tap.
